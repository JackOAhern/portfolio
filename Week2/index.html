Copper Mountain, CO

Location

Copper mountain is located in Summit County, Colorado, 75 miles west of Denver. Copper

stands only a few miles away from other well-known resorts, Breckenridge and Vail Mountain.

Thus, making Summit County one of the ski capitals of the country. The drive from Denver is only

about an hour and a half long, and the scenery along I-70 is an experience in itself.


Mountain Statistics

Base Elevation: 9,712 feet

Peak Elevation: 12,313 feet

Mountain Vertical: 2,601 feet

Skiable Area: 2,465 acres

Trails: 140+

  • Beginner - 21%
  • Intermediate - 25%

Mountain Highlights


Nationally Recgonized Terrain Park

Woodward at Copper is an award-winning terrain park that redefined what freestyle training looks like.

Woodward is one of the largest terrain parks in the United States, and offers off-snow training at the

Woodward barn as well. Summer Camps are hosted by Woodward for summer skiing and freestyle training.


Diverse Terrain


Copper is known for having a wide variety of terrain for all skill levels. The terrain gets more

and more difficult as you go from the West side to the East side of the mountain. There are also

multiple expert only bowl faces on the back side of the mountain.


Family Friendly

Copper Mountain is annually rated as one of the premiere family destinations. With endless activities

and events, there is always something to do for the little ones. Lodging at Copper is family focused

and there are many family packages available for discounted trips.
